What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  P = Proportional- Controls the turn rate of the robot by looking at the error. I = Integral- Adds up errors to eliminite small inaccuracies. D = Derivative- Can prepare a turn rate in advance by predicting an increase or decrease in the error. The process we did to figure out the best PID values was simple, but admittedly inefficient: Start out at 50/50/50/50, then do many test runs while increasing/decreasing each value to get a good idea of the exact effect each action had on the robots movement and tracking. After many trials and errors, we found the "sweet spot."
